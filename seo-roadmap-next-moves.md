# TulsaLaw SEO roadmap: next highest-leverage moves

## Purpose
Keep the site focused on quality local SEO gains that support consultations, lane clarity, and real Tulsa/Bixby relevance without expanding into thin content.

## Best next pages to add

1. **Tulsa Estate Planning FAQ**
   - Supports: `/estate-planning/`
   - Why next: high consumer intent, strong FAQ/featured-snippet potential, and useful for pre-consult qualification.
   - Focus: wills vs. trusts, POA timing, when to update documents, guardianship questions, what to bring.

2. **Tulsa Uncontested Divorce FAQ / Process Guide**
   - Supports: `/uncontested-divorce/`
   - Why next: improves CTR and consult quality by answering process, paperwork, timing, and fit questions.
   - Keep it useful, not generic. Should clearly route contested or high-conflict matters elsewhere.

3. **Tulsa LLC Formation vs. Filing Service**
   - Supports: `/llc-formation/`
   - Why next: strong commercial intent, strong differentiation angle, and helps qualify buyers who actually want attorney guidance.
   - Focus: operating agreement, ownership terms, authority, cleanup after DIY filing.

4. **South Tulsa / Bixby estate planning support page**
   - Supports: `/estate-planning/`
   - Only create if written as a genuinely useful service-area explainer, not a doorway page.
   - Focus: why nearby families choose a south Tulsa office, what documents to bring, and when local convenience matters.

## What not to build yet

- No bulk city pages for every suburb
- No generic personal injury blog roll
- No 24-page cluster dump from the audit all at once
- No thin "what is X" law content with no consult value

## Why some audit ideas were not implemented now

- **Dedicated Bixby doorway-style pages:** not justified without unique utility and would risk spam signals.
- **Large content cluster rollout:** too likely to dilute the site and create maintenance debt before the core lane architecture is fully strengthened.
- **Sitewide locality stuffing:** weaker than selective, credible local references tied to real service relevance.
